How This Works

From panic to paperwork — this toolkit guides you through the emergency filing process, step by step.

⚙️ What the App Does

  • 📝 Collects key info via a step-by-step form
  • 📍 Maps jurisdiction based on location and facts
  • 📄 Generates pre-filled legal documents (editable)
  • 📤 Lets users download, print, or email their filings
  • 🔐 Stores nothing. Your data never leaves your device unless you choose to export it.

📌 Designed for Families and Lawyers

Whether you're in crisis or standing by to help, this toolkit gives you a fast, private way to push back against unlawful detention and extrajudicial transfer. It's not legal advice. It's legal action, simplified.

🧭 How It Works

The HabeasNow Toolkit helps families and advocates rapidly generate emergency legal filings when someone has been detained without charges, especially if there’s a risk of secret transfer or disappearance.Here’s what the system creates:

  1. **Emergency Habeas Petition**
    - A complete filing under 28 U.S.C. § 2241, asserting violations of constitutional rights and requesting immediate judicial review. - Tailored to the facts you enter, including legal grounds and specific relief requested.
  2. **Declaration and Signature Page**
    - A formal statement under penalty of perjury, required for the court to act on your filing.
  3. **Temporary Restraining Order (TRO) – If Needed**
    - If your responses indicate risk of immediate transfer or disappearance, the toolkit will auto-generate a TRO draft. - This provides the court with a ready-to-sign request to halt movement or removal pending review.
  4. **Service Instructions**
    - Guidance on how and where to serve your filing, including contacts for the U.S. Attorney and Attorney General.
  5. **Filing Court Locator (Beta)**
    - Enter a ZIP code to help identify the appropriate U.S. District Court and generate venue information.

🧭 Our Legal Filing Flow

  1. Mock Legal Brief – A narrative summary of the situation, drafted in plain language.
  2. Federal Habeas Petition – Formatted to federal court standards (default is SDNY, but easily adaptable).
  3. Appellate Brief – If relief is denied, an appeal-ready draft is generated.
  4. Standalone TRO – A time-sensitive request for emergency relief.
  5. Proposed Order – A ready-to-sign document the judge can use to grant immediate protection.
  6. Notice of Hearing – Formal notification to the relevant parties and court.
