How This Works

From panic to paperwork — this toolkit guides you through the emergency filing process, step by step.

⚙️ What the App Does

  • 📝 Collects key info via a step-by-step form
  • 📍 Maps jurisdiction based on location and facts
  • 📄 Generates pre-filled legal documents (editable)
  • 📤 Lets users download, print, or email their filings
  • 🔐 Stores nothing. Your data never leaves your device unless you choose to export it.

📌 Designed for Families and Lawyers

Whether you're in crisis or standing by to help, this toolkit gives you a fast, private way to push back against unlawful detention and extrajudicial transfer. It's not legal advice. It's legal action, simplified.

🧭 How It Works

  1. Emergency Habeas Petition – A complete filing under 28 U.S.C. § 2241, asserting violations of constitutional rights and requesting immediate judicial review. Tailored to the facts you enter, including legal grounds and specific relief requested.
  2. Declaration and Signature Page – A formal statement under penalty of perjury, required for the court to act on your filing.
  3. Temporary Restraining Order (TRO) – If Needed – If your responses indicate risk of immediate transfer or disappearance, the toolkit will auto-generate a TRO draft. This provides the court with a ready-to-sign request to halt movement or removal pending review.
  4. Service Instructions – Guidance on how and where to serve your filing, including contacts for the U.S. Attorney and Attorney General.
  5. Filing Court Locator (Beta) – Enter a ZIP code to help identify the appropriate U.S. District Court and generate venue information.

🧯 What If ICE Claims They’re Not a Citizen?

In rare but documented cases, U.S. citizens have been mistakenly or deliberately treated as non-citizens and placed in ICE detention. When this happens, they may be routed through immigration court (EOIR) instead of the U.S. District Court system — and denied access to constitutional rights.

  • ⚠️ If ICE refuses to acknowledge valid proof of citizenship, a habeas petition may still be filed.
  • 📎 Include documents such as a passport, birth certificate, or Certificate of Citizenship.
  • 📞 Consider calling the EOIR or local immigration court only if the person is held in ICE custody — and always assert citizenship clearly and early.

If you believe ICE is holding someone unlawfully based on disputed status, file the emergency habeas and note the mistaken identity or denial of recognition as part of your filing.
